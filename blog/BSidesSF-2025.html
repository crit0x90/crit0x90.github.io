BSidesSF 2025

Why I Went

Around March of this year I decided that I needed to do a little in-person networking. I wanted to learn more about the broader infosec and tech industry and, given my proximity to the Bay Area, I decided to find a meet up in San Francisco. I did a little research an stumbled across BSides.

I went to BSides with learning about the industry and meeting people that work in it as my main goal. I didn't have any plans to participate in the CTF competitions and chose to attend panels when I needed a break from socializing instead.

What I Learned

General Career Advice

I wanted some diverse perspectives on my career prospects and how I could improve as a professional, so I sought career advice from as many people as I could. One common piece of advice from the weekend was that I needed more experience. In the past, I've always thought of experience as the thing that you get paid to do, but I was told that there were other things you could do that people would count as experience. For example, Bug Bounty programs, open source development, personal projects, and research can all contribute to your resume to fill experience gaps.

Another piece of advice that I got repeatedly was to create a targeted T-shaped resume. Targeted in that you should build your resume around the types of companies that you want to work for and T-shaped in that you should demonstrate broad experience in multiple areas and mastery in one area. For example, someone told me that I might try to show general knowledge of internal/external/application pentesting but then master one or two more niche skills like securing Kubernetes or Mac environments.

Tech Stuff

In between all the career stuff, I did find some time to see a few technical talks and talk security with some people. I meantioned that I was interested in learning k8s hacking to a guy and he told me to start with the "K8s the Hard Way" tutorials followed by the Kubenomicon. So far they've both been enlightening. I also saw a great talk by Breanne Boland about how pentesting AI agents isn't really that different from pentesting webapps.

Conclusion

BSidesSF was a great experience and I'll definitely go again next year!
